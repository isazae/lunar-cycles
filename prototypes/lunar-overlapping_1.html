All three waves are shown on the same time axis so you can see how they drift in and out of phase with each other. The gold and blue waves have nearly the same frequency, but the gold (synodic) is slightly slower — so it gradually falls behind the blue (tropical). Watch how they start aligned, then slowly separate. This drift is why full moons appear at different heights in the sky throughout the year. The purple wave (nodal) is so slow it appears nearly flat at this timescale — a gentle reminder that it takes 18.6 years to complete one oscillation.
