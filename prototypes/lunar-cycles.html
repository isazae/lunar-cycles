The wavy line shows the Moon's declination — how far north or south of the celestial equator it sits — oscillating every 27.3 days (the tropical month). The golden dots mark each full moon. Notice how the full moon dots slowly drift along the wave over the year: in winter they sit near the peaks (high in the sky), and by summer they've migrated to the troughs (low in the sky). This drift occurs because the phase cycle (29.5 days) is slightly longer than the declination cycle, causing them to slip out of sync by about 6 months.
