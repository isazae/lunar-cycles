Each wave represents a different cycle governing the Moon's behavior. The synodic wave (gold) shows the illumination cycle — peaks are full moons, troughs are new moons. The tropical wave (blue) shows declination — peaks are maximum northern position, troughs are maximum southern. The nodal wave (purple) shows how the amplitude of the tropical wave changes over 18.6 years — peaks are major standstills, troughs are minor standstills. Notice how the first two waves have nearly the same wavelength but are slightly offset — that ~2.2-day difference is what causes full moons to drift through different declination positions over the year.
